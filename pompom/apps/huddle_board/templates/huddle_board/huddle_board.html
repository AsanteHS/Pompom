PURPOSEFUL ROUNDING

Interview 2 RNs and 2 patients or their families and ask about purposeful rounding

Purposeful (hourly) rounding is a time to make a connection and involve the patient in a way to ensure quality, safety, and satisfaction.

 Ask the patient and family

a. Has the staff talked to you about Purposeful rounding?

b. What questions are we asking?

c. How often do you expect rounding?

d. What about at night when sleeping

Ask the staff

a. Why are we purposeful rounding?

b. What are we checking on?

BEDSIDE HANDOFF

Observe 2 bedside handoffs.
Assess the following components:

Handoff

✓ Room

a. Always done in room

b. Safety checks completed

c. Whiteboard updated

✓ Patient

a. Patient &amp; family invited to participate

b. Assessed &amp; verified IV, tubes, drains &amp; pumps

c. Cover plan of care, orders and test

d. Discuss pain management

e. Addressed questions and concerns

f. Reinforce the purposeful rounding process

✓ An hour prior to shift change

1. Do purposeful rounding to address needs and inform pt the unit is getting ready for handoff.

2. Charge nurses will attempt to minimize the number of nurses the oncoming nurse will need to get handoff from.

HAND HYGIENE

Select 2 Staff Members
Observe 5 opportunities for hand hygiene

Hand hygiene is the most effective means of preventing transmission of healthcare associated pathogens

✓ Soap and water

a. When hands are visibly soiled

b. After care of patient in enteric precautions

Wash hands together vigorously for 15 seconds covering all surfaces of hands and fingers

✘ Alcohol-based rubs or soap and water

1. Before touching a patient

2. Before clean/aseptic procedure

3. After body fluid exposure risk

4. After touching a patient

5. After touching patient surroundings

Rub hands together covering all surfaces of hands and fingers until hands are dry
